◈ PHASE I — INTRODUCTION
🚀 BILL FILING · STARFLEET ALPHA SEQUENCE
A member of either chamber drafts and files the bill. The bill receives a number (HB### or SB###), is read by title only for the First Reading, and is referred to a standing committee by the Speaker (House) or Lieutenant Governor (Senate). The originating chamber determines the bill's primary pathway.
▼
ORIGINATING CHAMBER DETERMINES PRIMARY PATH
◈ PHASE II — COMMITTEE REVIEW (BOTH CHAMBERS)
⚠ POINT OF ORDER
🏛️ HOUSE STANDING COMMITTEE
Speaker refers bill to appropriate committee. Committee holds a public hearing — witnesses testify for/against. Committee may adopt a substitute (rewritten) bill. Members vote to report the bill favorably, with amendments, or unfavorably to the full House.

🏛️ SENATE STANDING COMMITTEE
Lt. Governor refers bill to committee. Public hearing held; substitute bills may be offered. Committee votes to report favorably to full Senate. Unlike the House, Senate committee chairs wield significant scheduling power.

◈ PHASE IV — FLOOR DEBATE & PASSAGE (ORIGINATING CHAMBER)
▼
⚠ POINT OF ORDER
🎙️ HOUSE FLOOR — 2ND & 3RD READINGS
2nd Reading: Full debate. Members offer floor amendments (must be germane). Any member may raise a Point of Order against procedure or rule violations — sustained POOs can kill the bill or table an amendment. 3rd Reading: Final vote; only minor corrections allowed. Requires simple majority (76/150).

✅ HOUSE PASSAGE
Bill passes by majority vote (76 of 150). Officially engrossed — clean copy prepared. Transmitted to Senate via official message.

🎙️ SENATE FLOOR — 2ND & 3RD READINGS
2nd Reading: Floor debate; amendments offered. A blocking motion may require 3/5 vote (19/31) to proceed. Members may raise Points of Order. 3rd Reading: Final vote on passage. Simple majority required (16/31). Lt. Gov. presides and rules on procedural questions.

◈ PHASE VI — CONFERENCE (IF CHAMBERS DISAGREE)
▼
⚠ POINT OF ORDER
🤝 CONFERENCE COMMITTEE · JOINT BICAMERAL
Each chamber appoints conferees (5 from House, 5 from Senate) to negotiate differences. The Conference Committee may only address matters in genuine disagreement — it cannot introduce new subjects outside the scope of the House/Senate versions. Both chambers must adopt the conference report by majority vote with no further amendments allowed.

BOTH CHAMBERS ADOPT · BILL MOVES TO ENROLLMENT
📜 ENROLLMENT & ENGROSSMENT · FINAL CLEAN COPY
An official clean copy of the final bill (incorporating all amendments) is prepared — this is the enrolled bill. Signed by the Speaker of the House and the Lieutenant Governor. Transmitted to the Governor's office.
◈ PHASE VII — EXECUTIVE ACTION · GOVERNOR'S DESK
▼
🏛️ GOVERNOR'S REVIEW · COMMAND DECISION
Governor has 10 days (excluding Sundays) to act while the Legislature is in session, or 20 days after adjournment. Three options: Sign into law, allow to become law without signature (no action), or Veto. The Governor may also use a line-item veto on appropriations bills only.
▼
GOVERNOR CHOOSES ONE OF THREE OUTCOMES
✅ SIGN
Governor signs. Bill becomes law on effective date.
🕐 NO ACTION
No signature within time limit = automatically becomes law (in session). Returned unsigned after adjournment = pocket veto (bill dies).
❌ VETO
Returned to Legislature. Override requires 2/3 vote in each chamber (100 House / 21 Senate). Override succeeds → law. Fails → bill dies.
▼
MISSION ACCOMPLISHED · ENROLLED BILL ENACTED
⚖️ ENACTED INTO TEXAS LAW · MISSION COMPLETE
Bill becomes a Texas Act — assigned a chapter number and codified into the Texas Statutes. Filed with the Secretary of State.

Effective date options:
• Standard: September 1st following passage
• Immediate effect: Requires a 2/3 vote in both chambers (emergency clause)
• Specific date: As stated in the bill text
